About Oklahoma

Oklahoma, often referred to as the Sooner State, is located in the South Central region of the United States. Known for its diverse landscapes, rich Native American heritage, and strong agricultural and energy sectors, Oklahoma is a land of opportunity and cultural significance. From bustling cities to expansive plains, Oklahoma offers a unique blend of history, innovation, and natural beauty.

Fun Facts

  • Oklahoma has more man-made lakes than any other state in the U.S.
  • Home to 39 Native American tribes, Oklahoma has a rich Native American heritage.
  • The state's name comes from the Choctaw words "okla" and "humma," meaning "red people."

State Bird: Scissor-tailed Flycatcher

The Scissor-tailed Flycatcher, with its long, graceful tail, is the state bird of Oklahoma. It is known for its elegant flight and is commonly found across the state.
